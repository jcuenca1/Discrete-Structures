  • Question 1:

    • In an election, there are 4 Democrats, 6 Republicans, and 2 Libertarians running for president. What is the probability that if you randomly choose a candidate, you will choose a Republican?

  • Answer 1:
    The same space is the total amount of candidates running for office. This can be calculated by adding 4 + 6 + 2, which equals 12 candidates total. There are 4 events where there would be a Republican candidate chosen, as there are 4 Republican candidates.

    Therefore, the probability you will choose a Republican candidate is 4 / 12 or 1 / 3.

  • Question 2:

    • A survey distributed to 30 students asks the question, "Out of the following colors, red, orange, yellow, green, blue, or purple, which is your favorite?" 6 students choose red, 1 student chose yellow, 2 students choose orange, and 10 students choose green. Assuming every student only chose one color, what is the probaility that a student chose a cool color (green, blue, or purple)?

  • Answer 2:
    We first must acknowledge that there is missing information because the responses did not include the students who voted for purple, nor. Even though not all of the results of the cool colors are listed, we can find the the complement of the warm colors (red, orange, and yellow).
    The event of getting a warm color is 9 (6 + 1 + 2). The event of getting a cool color can be calculated by subtracting the event of a warm color from the sample space (the 30 students). Therefore, the event of getting a cool color is 21(30 - 9).

    Therefore, the probability of a student choosing a cool color is 21 / 30.

  • Question 3:

    • If you flip a coin and roll a die, what is the probability that you get heads and roll a 4?

  • Answer 3:
    In this problem, flipping a coin and rolling a die are two independent events. In the case of independent events, we use the elementary rule of multiplication. T he probability that you get heads is 1 / 2 and the probability that you roll a 4 is 1 / 6. To find the probability of both of these events, you must multiply 1 / 2 by 1 / 6.

    Therefore, the probability of flipping heads and rolling a 4 is 1 / 12 (1 / 2 * 1 / 6).

  • Question 4:

    • Using a standard deck of cards, what is the probability that you pick or odd number card that is black? (don't count an ace as a 1)

  • Answer 4:
    In this question, we notice we can't simply add the the probability of getting an odd number card or a black card because we would be counting the cards that are both odd numbers and black. We use the general addition rule to solve this problem. The probability that we choose an odd number card is 16 / 52 and the probability of choosing a black card is 26 / 52. The probability that you choose a card that is an odd number and is black is 8 / 52. Using the general addition rule, we can calculate the probability of choosing a card that is an odd number or black by adding the probability of choosing an odd number card (16 / 52) and the probability of choosing a black card (26 / 52), and then subtracting the probability of choosing a card that is an odd number and black (8 / 52).

    Therefore, the probability the of choosing a card that is an odd number or black is 34 / 52 or 17 / 26.
