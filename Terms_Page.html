Here are the main terms used in the Chapter:

Bernoulli trial - an experiment in which there are only two possible outcomes

Complement (of an event) - the event consisting of all the outcomes that do not occur in E. Can be calculated by S - E Can also be represented by E’

Disjointness - two or more events are disjoint if the outcomes associated with one event are not present in the outcomes of any other events

Event - a specific set of outcomes (ex. In a coin tossing question, the event would be “tossing a head”)

Independent - Event 1 and Event 2 are independent of each other if the outcome of any one of these events does not in any way impact or influence the outcome of the other event

Probability Distribution - determines the probabilities of all possible outcomes over a specified sample space.

Sample space (of the event) - the set of all possible outcomes
